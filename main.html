The Vision

The long-term goal of these research areas is to responsibly automate physics, harnessing the power of AI to advance our understanding of the physical world while ensuring safety and alignment with human values.

Evaluations and Benchmarks for Physics Agents

This research area focuses on developing new evaluation methods and benchmarks for physics agents. The goal is to use physics as a ground truth for AI, providing a solid foundation for assessing the performance and capabilities of these AI agents. New interpretability methods will also be explored.

Autonomous Computational Physics Agents

This research direction explores the development of autonomous computational physics agents, with a special emphasis on physics simulations. They will be sandboxed in simulations, allowing for safe exploration and demonstration of their capabilities.

Safety of Science-Capable LM Agents

The safety of language model (LM) agents capable of scientific reasoning is important. This research area will investigate methods to ensure the alignment and safety of actions taken by AI physics agents. In simulated environments, many safety and alignment issues can be explored without real-world consequences.

Scalable Oversight for Super- Alignment

Funded by the OpenAI, this research area aims to develop scalable oversight mechanisms for super alignment, using physics as a ground truth. The goal is to ensure that as AI systems become more powerful, they remain aligned with human values and intentions.
